============================================================
PROMPT D6 — Monte Carlo Results Unreliable
============================================================

PROMPT --- Use when: Monte Carlo results change too much between
runs, or P10/P90 range is too wide

**I am debugging Monte Carlo simulation results in my digital twin math
engine.**

Problem: [describe --- e.g. \"P10=120 units and P90=280 units ---
range is too wide\"

OR \"Running same simulation twice gives very different P50\"]

My Monte Carlo function:

[PASTE your run_monte_carlo function]

My input distributions:

[PASTE the distributions you are using for cycle times, demand,
breakdowns]

Please:

1. Check if random seeds are being set correctly (each trial needs a
different seed)

2. Check if n_trials=10000 is enough --- calculate the standard error
of the mean

3. Check if input distributions are calibrated correctly:

- CV for cycle times should be 0.08--0.25 for factory machines

- If CV is > 0.5, distributions are too variable and results will be
unreliable

4. Check if the Weibull parameters for breakdowns are reasonable for
manufacturing

5. Check if the P10/P90 range makes physical sense:

- A range of more than ±30% of P50 suggests input distributions are
too wide

6. Show me corrected distribution parameters and explain why they are
more realistic
